Promoting Mental Well-being for All

Mental health is not merely the absence of mental illness but a state of emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It influences how people think, feel, and act, and determines how they handle stress, make decisions, and maintain relationships. Our research shows that awareness and early intervention can significantly reduce long-term mental health challenges.

Fact: The World Health Organization (WHO) reports that one in every eight people globally lives with a mental disorder, yet over 70% of them do not receive proper treatment due to lack of awareness.

Major Mental Health Concerns by Age Group

Children and Teenagers

Face issues such as academic pressure, bullying, and lack of emotional communication. Early counseling and healthy family interactions help in building resilience.

Adults

Stress related to work, relationships, or finances often leads to anxiety or burnout. Maintaining a work-life balance and seeking therapy when needed are essential coping strategies.

Older Adults

May experience loneliness, memory loss, or depression due to health decline or social isolation. Regular social engagement and mental exercises can promote cognitive health.

Gender Perspective

Women often experience postnatal depression and hormonal mood changes, whereas men are more prone to stress-related disorders due to societal expectations of strength and control.

Practices for Better Mental Health

  • Maintain regular sleep and a balanced routine.
  • Engage in mindfulness, meditation, or journaling.
  • Stay connected with family and supportive communities.
  • Seek professional help when emotional distress becomes overwhelming.
  • Limit screen time and exposure to negative online content.
Did You Know? Studies show that sharing emotions with trusted people can reduce stress levels by nearly 40% and improve overall emotional stability.
